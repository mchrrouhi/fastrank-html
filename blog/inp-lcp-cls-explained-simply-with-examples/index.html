INP, LCP & CLS Explained Simply (with Examples)

A practical, non‑technical breakdown of what INP, LCP and CLS measure, why they matter for UX, SEO & ads, and how to fix the most common issues.

1. Why Core Web Vitals exist

Google needed simple, user‑centric metrics: fastest meaningful paint (LCP), layout stability (CLS), interaction responsiveness (INP). They correlate with satisfaction & conversion.

2. LCP in plain English

Time until the main thing (hero, product image, heading) appears clearly. Slow LCP: huge images, blocking CSS/JS, slow server.

3. CLS & layout jump

Measures unexpected movement. Caused by images without reserved space, late banners, dynamic widgets, font swaps altering dimensions.

4. INP & interaction lag

Latency between user action and next paint. High INP: long JS tasks, heavy listeners, over‑engineered components.

5. Benchmarks

6. Common problems

  • LCP: Oversized hero; multiple font & animation loads; absent caching.
  • CLS: No width/height; injected consent bars; late review widgets.
  • INP: App/plugin script bloat; large synchronous bundles; unused listeners.

7. Measuring

  • PageSpeed Insights (lab + field)
  • Search Console CWV report (field only)
  • Chrome DevTools Performance & Coverage

8. Quick wins

  • Compress & preload hero image; convert to WebP.
  • Reserve layout slots for images, ads, embeds.
  • Defer non‑critical scripts; split long tasks.
  • Host fonts locally; limit weights; use font-display:swap.

9. Overwhelmed?

Phase changes: baseline → hero & layout → script deferral → interaction cleanup → field validation. If resource constrained, focus on pages driving revenue or organic entry.
